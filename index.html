Accountability

1. I define accountability as taking ownership of my actions and following through on what I commit to.

2. I learned this value through leadership roles, team projects, and situations where people depended on me to deliver.

3. Accountability is important because it builds credibility and shows others they can rely on me.

4. When I fail to be accountable, I damage trust, weaken team performance, and limit my own growth.

Collaboration

1. I define collaboration as working with others in a way that values shared ideas, active listening, and collective success.

2. I developed this value through group projects, student leadership, and experiences where teamwork produced better outcomes than individual effort.

3. Collaboration is important because it brings diverse strengths together and creates solutions that no one person could achieve alone.

4. When I fail to collaborate, I contribute to miscommunication, duplicated work, and a breakdown in team trust.

Integrity

1. I see integrity as acting honestly and consistently with my values, even when it is difficult.

2. I developed this value through academic expectations, leadership responsibilities, and moments where doing the right thing required courage.

3. Integrity is important because it shapes my reputation and determines whether people feel they can trust me.

4. When I do not uphold integrity, I risk losing credibility, damaging relationships, and creating long-term consequences that are hard to repair.

Empathy

1. I define empathy as my ability to understand what others are feeling and respond with genuine care.

2. I developed this value through peer mentoring, group work, and moments where listening mattered more than speaking.

3. Empathy is important to me because it helps build trust and makes people feel safe sharing their experiences.

4. When I fail to practice empathy, I risk misreading situations, damaging relationships, and making others feel unseen or dismissed.
